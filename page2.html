What is the difference in Ramen between miso and shoyu?

The primary difference between miso and shoyu ramen is the tare (seasoning base), which determines the flavor, color, and thickness. Miso ramen uses fermented bean paste for a rich, thick, and savory soup. Shoyu ramen uses soy sauce, resulting in a lighter, clear brown broth with a savory, tangy, and refined flavor.
